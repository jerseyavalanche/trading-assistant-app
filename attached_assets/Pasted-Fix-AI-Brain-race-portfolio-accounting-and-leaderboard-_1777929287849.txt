Fix AI Brain race portfolio accounting and leaderboard ranking.

Problem:
Buys are currently being counted as immediate losses. That is wrong. A BUY should convert cash into an open position, not reduce PnL.

Goal:
Implement isolated virtual sub-portfolios per bot. Race scoring must come from these virtual portfolios, not shared broker/account balances.

Required portfolio fields per bot:
- starting_budget
- cash
- open_positions[]
- realized_pnl
- unrealized_pnl
- total_pnl
- total_pnl_pct
- total_equity

Open position fields:
- symbol
- qty
- notional
- avg_entry_price

- market_value
- unrealized_pnl

BUY behavior:
- Reduce that bot’s cash by filled notional plus fees if fees exist.
- Create or update an open position.
- Update qty/notional/avg_entry_price/cost_basis.
- Do NOT count the buy amount as a loss.
- Immediately after buying $125 from a $250 budget, bot should show:
  - cash ≈ 125
  - open_position_value ≈ 125
  - unrealized_pnl ≈ 0
  - realized_pnl = 0
  - total_pnl ≈ 0
  - total_equity ≈ 250

SELL behavior:
- Increase bot cash by proceeds minus fees if fees exist.
- Reduce or close the open position.
- Move closed-position PnL into realized_pnl.
- Remove closed positions or mark qty=0 and exclude from active open_positions.

Mark-to-market:
- Every race cycle, use latest price per symbol.
- Revalue each bot’s open positions.
- Update:
  - unrealized_pnl = sum(position market_value - cost_basis)
  - total_equity = cash + sum(open position market_value)
  - total_pnl = total_equity - starting_budget
  - total_pnl_pct = total_pnl / starting_budget * 100

Leaderboard:
- Stop ranking by current net_pnl if it treats buys as losses.
- Rank by total_equity descending.
- Or rank by total_pnl_pct descending.
- Ensure bots holding positions are not punished just because cash went down.
